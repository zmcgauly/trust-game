What the proposer sends will be multiplied by a multiplier. The multiplier can be either {{ low_multiplier }} or {{ large_multiplier }} and will be established each period by fair odds.

Suppose the proposer sends some number of points and the multiplier has been applied.

After multiplication, the responder has:

\[ \text{points available to responder} = \text{points sent by proposer} \times \text{multiplier} \]

If the responder returns points to the proposer, the earnings for that round are calculated as follows:

Proposer's Earnings

\[ \text{proposer earnings} = 20 - \text{points sent by proposer} + \text{points returned by responder} \]

Responder's Earnings

\[ \text{responder earnings} = \text{points available to responder} - \text{points returned by responder} \]

Example

Suppose the proposer sends 10 points and the multiplier is 3.

The responder receives:

\[ 10 \times 3 = 30 \text{ points} \]

If the responder returns 12 points, then:

Proposer's earnings

\[ 20 - 10 + 12 = 22 \text{ points} \]

Responder's earnings

\[ 30 - 12 = 18 \text{ points} \]

If this round is selected for payment, these point totals will be converted into cash according to the payment procedure described at the end of the session.
