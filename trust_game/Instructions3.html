In each round, the proposer receives 20 points. The proposer chooses how many whole points, from 0 to 20, to send to the responder.

Points sent by the proposer are multiplied before the responder makes a decision. In some rounds, the multiplier is fixed at 3. In other rounds, the multiplier is randomly either 3 or 6, with an equal chance of each outcome. You will not know which you are in during the experiment.

In a random multiplier round, the proposer will not know whether the multiplier is 3 or 6 before making the sending decision. The responder sees the actual multiplier and the resulting number of available points before choosing how much to send back.

The responder, however, will see how many points the proposer sent and the total points available after multiplication. The responder then chooses how many whole points to send back to the proposer. The responder can send back any whole number from 0 up to the total points available.
