At the beginning of every round, the proposer receives an endowment of 20 points.

The proposer decides how many whole points, from 0 to 20, to send to the responder.

The number of points sent is multiplied before the responder makes a decision.

What the proposer sends will be multiplied by a multiplier. The multiplier can be either {{ low_multiplier }} or {{ large_multiplier }} and will be established each period by fair odds.

After the multiplier has been applied, the responder observes:

  • the number of points sent by the proposer,
  • the total number of points available.

The responder then decides how many whole points to return to the proposer. The responder may return any whole number from 0 up to the total number of points available after multiplication.
